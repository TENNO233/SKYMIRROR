## Page 3

As Singapore celebrates its 60th year of
independence, we at the Land Transport
Authority (LTA) mark three decades of
service. For half of Singapore’s journey
as a nation, LTA has played a key role in
keeping Singapore moving, connecting
communities, supporting economic activity
and transforming our transport landscape.
Today, our public transport system
supports 7.5 million trips each day across
our network of nine Mass Rapid Transit
(MRT) and Light Rail Transit (LRT) lines,
and 366 bus services. Over 9,500 lane-
kilometres of roads form Singapore’s
extensive road infrastructure, built,
managed and maintained by LTA. Our
cycling path network is expanding to reach
1,300km by 2030, as we steadily progress
towards our Land Transport Master Plan
(LTMP) 2040 vision of 20 minute towns and
45-minute city.
Building for Tomorrow
The past year saw the opening of the
Thomson-East Coast Line 4, Punggol
Coast station, Hume station and Teck
Lee LRT station, enhancing connectivity
and expanding our network’s reach.
We are pressing ahead with ambitious
plans, including investing an additional
$1 billion over the next five years
to strengthen rail capabilities and
reliability. Other expansions include
building the Jurong Region Line and
Cross Island Line, while the Circle Line
will complete its loop by 2026.
We are not stopping there. Feasibility
studies have begun for possible Tengah
and Seletar Lines, which could serve
more than 400,000 households. The Johor
Bahru – Singapore Rapid Transit Link,
targeted for completion in December 2026,
will add a vital cross-border connection.
The Bus Connectivity Enhancement
Programme (BCEP), launched in July 2024,
has improved connectivity for commuters.
With close to $1 billion to be invested over
eight years, it has seen the introduction
of 10 new bus services and enhancement
of over 40 existing routes. In 2024, we
also opened the Buangkok Integrated
Transport Hub, Tengah Bus Interchange
and Sengkang West Bus Depot, providing
greater accessibility and convenience.

## Page 4

Our rail and bus systems are strongly
supported by innovation. We opened the
Singapore Rail Test Centre in March, and
by 2026, the East Coast Integrated Depot
will begin operations, bringing three rail
lines and bus operations under one roof
for greater efficiency.
We reviewed the Point-to-Point Transport
Regulatory Framework to better support
commuters who require more direct
transport options, and to ensure that the
taxi and private hire car industry remains
stable and sustainable.
Fostering Enjoyable Commutes
Beyond infrastructure, improving the
overall commuting experience on public
transport remains a key priority.
We reached a milestone last year
when our Graciousness campaign and
Thoughtful Bunch celebrated their 10
th
anniversary of promoting considerate
behaviour on public transport.
During the Caring Commuters Week,
we recognised 10 exemplary individuals
who demonstrated exceptional kindness
to fellow commuters.
Commuters now enjoy seamless
ticketing and travel-card services
through SimplyGo. The consolidation of
TransitLink and EZ-Link under SimplyGo
Pte. Ltd. allows us to drive innovation
in ticketing and transport payment
solutions.
The enhanced Travel Smart Journeys
programme offers greater savings and
travel options for commuters in the
northeast region. They can either shift
their morning travel time, or switch to
bus services from selected North East
Line MRT and LRT stations during the
morning peak.
Moving Forward Sustainably
The Master Plan for the North-South
Corridor (NSC) is being developed through
extensive public consultation. More
than just a major transport corridor,
the NSC has the potential to realign
our commuting journey and community
spaces when it is completed. Our Friendly
Streets initiative will extend to all towns
by 2030, making everyday journeys more
welcoming and safer for all pedestrians.
New Personal Mobility Aid regulations
that will come into effect in 2026 will
enhance path safety, while over 50
community-led placemaking projects
are bringing art and vibrancy to our
communities.
Sustainability remains at the core of our
plans. Over 24,000 EV charging points have
been installed, and 420 new electric public
buses will be deployed by early 2026.
Shaping Future Mobility
Advanced technology continues to shape
our transport strategy. By the end of this
year, LTA will begin deploying autonomous
mini-shuttles on short, fixed routes
to improve accessibility in HDB towns
located further away from the city. This
is part of LTA’s efforts to make public
transport an attractive alternative to
private vehicles. We are also enhancing
bus-train connectivity and improving first-
and-last-mile connectivity to support a
car-lite Singapore.
On the private transport front, about
700,000 or 70% of vehicles have installed
their On-Board Units as of August 2025.
Motorists are now able to receive real-
time traffic information such as bus lane
operations, school zones and alerts on
flash floods.
Our Shared Journey
As Singaporeans, we can take pride in our
public transport system that has grown
in tandem with our nation’s progress.
Our journey is not just about meeting
milestones or achieving numbers—it is
about delivering meaningful service to the
people we serve.
Our progress over the 30 years has been
made possible with the dedication of our
transport workers, unions and partners,
and the trust of the public. The road
ahead will bring new challenges, and
exciting opportunities to reimagine and
reshape Singapore’s transport landscape.
We remain committed to building a
sustainable, connected and inclusive
transport system – one that keeps your
world moving, and moves with you.
Thank you for being part of this journey -
we look forward to serving you better in
the years ahead.
